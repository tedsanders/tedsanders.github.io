I’m another human being, just like you.


My goals are to understand the world, improve it, and find a happy place within it.


As a volunteer, I’ve tried to distribute knowledge:

* Co-hosted the science-comedy podcast [Goggles Optional](http://gogglesoptional.com/) at Stanford for 2.5 years
* Authored [Wikipedia](https://www.wikipedia.org/) articles on [the LaAlO3/SrTiO3 interface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanthanum_aluminate-strontium_titanate_interface), [complex oxides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complex_oxide), and other topics
* Traded on [scientific prediction markets](http://scicast.org/)
* Encouraged kids in science through [Boys and Girls Club](https://www.bgcp.org/#mission), [Science Bus](https://oso.stanford.edu/student-outreach/5-science-bus), [Science Bowl](http://science.energy.gov/wdts/nsb/), [Maker Faire](http://makerfaire.com/), [Bay Area Science Festival](http://www.bayareascience.org/), [FoldScope](http://www.foldscope.com/), [Techbridge Girls](http://www.techbridgegirls.org/), [Bay Area Scientists in Schools](http://www.crscience.org/volunteers/aboutbasis), [San Francisco Science Fair](http://www.sfbasf.org/), [Synopsis Science & Technology Championship](https://science-fair.org/), [SURA](http://sura.stanford.edu/), [UC Berkeley poster judging](http://engineering.berkeley.edu/students/current-undergraduates/student-research/undergraduate-research-poster-sessions), [Engineers Week](https://sites.google.com/site/berkeleyengineersweek/), [CAL Prep](http://cep.berkeley.edu/cal-prep), Stanford NOMS, [AARC Tutoring](https://undergrad.stanford.edu/advising/getting-started/advising-student-athletes/aarc-and-other-campus-tutorial-resources), and [Dinner with a Scientist](http://science.ousd.org/dinner.htm)
* These efforts have been covered by [Science](http://www.sciencemag.org/careers/2016/11/scientists-ride-podcasting-wave), [Nature](http://www.nature.com/news/the-power-of-prediction-markets-1.20820), [The White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/2010/05/12/national-lab-day-dinner-with-a-scientist), and [Vox](http://www.vox.com/2016/7/14/12016710/science-challeges-research-funding-peer-review-process)

As a scientist, I’ve tried to generate new knowledge:

+ In my PhD, I advanced humanity's understanding of physics, with the aim of one day building radically better computers. Toward this goal, I synthesized new materials that had never before existed in the universe (as far as we know) and measured their electronic & magnetic properties to test our theories of solid state physics. 
+ Synthesized crystals used to build SketchFET, the world’s first nanoscale, GHz-speed writable transistor (for applications molecular sensing)
+ Published on magnetic thin films, 2D electron gases, writeable nanotransistors, organic spintronics, high-temperature superconductors, nanowire solar cells, and the cost-effectiveness of batteries to stabilize solar intermittency

As a management consultant, I've tried to turn knowledge into impact:

+ Corporate strategy
+ Product strategy
+ New tech ventures
+ Market entry
+ Sales & marketing

See my resume [here]() and my LinkedIn [here](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tedsanders/).

10% of the money I earn goes to [Against Malaria Foundation](https://www.againstmalaria.com/), the world’s most effective charity as evaluated by [GiveWell](http://www.givewell.org/).
